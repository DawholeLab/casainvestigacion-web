“Attention is the rarest and purest form of generosity.” — Simone Weil

Reality rarely speaks with a single voice. Between institutions and citizens, between organizations and people, between decisions and their consequences, there are distances that cannot be resolved with information alone.

To research is to understand those distances.

At La Casa de Investigación we study how people perceive, interpret, feel, remember and act. We listen before concluding. We contrast before asserting. We seek to understand rather than to confirm our own hypotheses.

We believe every important decision deserves a rigorous understanding of the human reality it intends to transform.

We do not produce data to fill reports. We produce knowledge to reduce uncertainty, broaden understanding and improve the quality of decisions.

Understanding is the precondition for acting intelligently.

Our approach

Research as the restitution of reality

Every organization builds, over time, a representation of reality. That representation guides its decisions, but it can also end up distancing it from people's concrete experience.

Research begins there.

To research is to suspend one's own certainties in order to understand the world again from the standpoint of those who live it. It is to reconstruct how citizens, clients, consumers, users or audiences perceive, interpret and make sense of what they interact with.

We do not merely look for opinions. We seek to understand the psychological, cultural, social and systemic processes that make them possible.

That is why we do not simply deliver data. We restore a deeper understanding of reality, capable of reducing uncertainty, challenging assumptions and raising the quality of decisions.

To achieve this we have developed proprietary methodologies that integrate psychology, sociology, anthropology and systems thinking into a single analytical framework.

Because every good decision begins with a better understanding of reality.

The teams

Every study requires a different team. Excellence lies in knowing how to configure it.

We do not believe in rigid structures or permanent teams for every challenge. Each project demands a specific combination of disciplinary experience, sector knowledge and international track record. That is why we assemble tailor-made teams, integrating complementary profiles according to the nature of the research.

Project leadership

Psychologists, sociologists, anthropologists and communication specialists with more than fifteen years leading complex research in multiple countries and supporting highly demanding political, institutional and corporate processes.

Senior analysis

Researchers with doctoral training and academic careers at universities such as Berkeley, Cambridge, MIT, Universidad de Barcelona, Complutense de Madrid, Rey Juan Carlos, UNAM, Universidad Católica de Chile, University of Surrey and Iberoamericana, among others.

Fieldwork

Moderators, ethnographers and social researchers with extensive experience in qualitative research and training in anthropology, psychology, sociology and communication, from institutions such as ENAH, UNAM, UAM, Universidad de Chile, Universidad de Buenos Aires and Iberoamericana.

Specialized analysis
